Among the folkloric tales of the city of Chelm, describes the wisest leaders of their community, foolishly, trying to capture the moonlight (אור הירח, grammaticly conjugated in the adjacent picture), as it reflects off the water in a barrel.

The story continues to give lessons, beyond the time of its origin. As people continue to capture, preserve, and re-observe the light lost to history, through film, digital mediums, and emrging technoligies of the future, it is yet a more advanced method, than that of the Chelmites attempts to do so.

Continuing with the moonlight analogy, the light that reflects off the surface of water, reveals a single points perspective of the light source, and consequently, reflects it in a way, that is unique to its reflection point, relative to the observer's position.
