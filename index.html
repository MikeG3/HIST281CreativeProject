European-Jewish folklore has tales about the foolish inhabitants of the city of Chelm. One story describes the wisest leaders of their community, attempting to capture moonlight in a barrel, that is reflected off of the water contained inside of it, in order to save it for the future.

The story continues to give lessons, beyond the time of its origin. People continue to capture, preserve, and re-observe the light lost to history, through film, digital mediums, and emerging technologies, of modern times. These efforts are gradual advancements, from the attempts of the Chelmites.

Continuing with the moonlight analogy, the light that reflects off the surface of water, is not only distorted by the water, it reveals a single point's perspective of the light source. Consequently, the perception of the light depends on the position of both the barrel, and the observer.
